Sketch narrative — Sunflower Bloom Clock

Context: This clock shows time through the rhythm of a sunflower. Each of the 12 petals represents an hour, opening and closing every ten seconds to reflect steady breathing and natural cycles. It is designed for mindfulness and calm focus while studying or meditating.

  • Who it is for: Students or anyone who wants a calm, meditative way to visualize time.
  • Design decisions: Chose golden tones inspired by sunlight and a smooth bloom animation to mimic slow breathing. Added a subtle gradient background that shifts softly to match the passing of time.
  • Feedback and iteration: Classmates said the colors were too sharp and transitions too fast. I slowed down the bloom speed and warmed the palette for a more soothing effect.
  • Future work: Add a hover highlight to show the current hour and a smoother transition from day to dusk lighting.

Sketch narrative — Track Tempo Clock

Context: This clock visualizes time through motion on a running track. Each lane represents seconds, minutes, or hours, and a dot runs around the track to show progression. The design connects time to physical movement, emphasizing rhythm and endurance.

  • Who it is for: People who enjoy athletics, running, or performance-based visuals where time feels dynamic and continuous.
  • Design decisions: Three lanes for seconds, minutes, and hours. Each with its own color and motion speed. A dark background and neon contrast to evoke a stadium or night run. Added easing to make the pace natural.
  • Feedback and iteration: Classmates mentioned the track edges felt cluttered and that the contrast was too high. I simplified the borders, reduced the glow, and added a small hover effect to toggle the timezone for usability.
  • Future work: Introduce subtle ambient sound or color changes that reflect different times of day for a more immersive atmosphere.

Sketch narrative — Emoji Runner Clock (Final)

Context: Time and emotion move together. A small runner loops the dial while its face changes with effort. The background shifts with intensity so you feel the state of the moment.

  • Who it is for: Anyone who wants a playful yet honest view of time, where mood and pace are visible.
  • Design decisions: Emoji faces to show feeling at a glance. A soft trail to track motion. A simple HUD with time, pace, fatigue, and effort so the idea stays readable.
  • Feedback and iteration: Classmates said the layout felt busy. I simplified the HUD, tuned color balance, and added a pulse toggle for calmer animation.
  • Future work: Add a subtle sound pulse that follows effort and a low contrast night theme.
